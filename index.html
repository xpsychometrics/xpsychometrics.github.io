Our Research

Cognitive Diagnosis Models

Developing rigorous psychometric toolsets grounded in item response theory and latent class analysis to support personalized learning and data-informed instructional decision-making.

Item Response Theory

Advancing IRT methodologies and applications to improve the design, implementation, and interpretation of assessments across traditional and interactive platforms.

Machine Learning & AI

Applying machine learning and artificial intelligence techniques to enhance measurement practices in education and psychology.

Latent Class Analysis

Utilizing latent class analysis and factor analysis methods to uncover underlying structures in psychological and educational data.

Scale Development

Developing and validating surveys and scales with rigorous psychometric methods to ensure reliability and validity of test scores.

Software Development

Creating and maintaining open-source software packages, including the GDINA R package, to support psychometric research and practice.

Our Team

Dr. Wenchao Ma

Associate Professor
The American Guidance Service Inc. and John P. Yackel Professor in Educational Assessment and Measurement

Dr. Ma's research centers on the development of innovative psychometric methods to enhance measurement practices in education and psychology, along with the application of measurement techniques to survey and scale development and validation.

A central focus of his recent methodological work is cognitive diagnosis models (CDMs), which are grounded in item response theory (IRT) and latent class analysis. His goal is to develop rigorous psychometric toolsets that support personalized learning and data-informed instructional decision-making.
